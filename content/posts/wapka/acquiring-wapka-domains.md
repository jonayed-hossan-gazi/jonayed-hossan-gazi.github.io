Most founder stories start with "I bought the domain." Mine started with "I couldn't."

---

## The .com that wouldn't sell

The original Wapka ran on wapka.com and wapka.mobi. When the platform shut down, those domains stayed with the original owner. I reached out early in the rebuild — 2018 or 2019 — and made an offer.

The response: $25,000 per domain.

I had saved around $4,000 from small projects, platform ad revenue, and community contributions. I was a teenager. Twenty-five thousand dollars was not a number I could reach. The negotiation ended there.

Most people would have walked away. A platform without its original domain is a platform with a branding problem. But I didn't have the luxury of a perfect start. I had to build the brand from what I could reach.

---

## The alternative strategy

Instead of one premium domain, I bought the reachable alternatives — one at a time, as funds allowed.

**wapka.co** came first. Then **wapka.site**. Then **wapka.website**. Each acquisition was a small victory. Each one said: the brand is alive, even if the .com isn't ours yet.

| Domain | Acquired |
|---|---|
| wapka.website | August 2018 |
| wapka.site | April 2019 |

These weren't ideal. A .co or .site doesn't carry the same authority as a .com. But they work. They resolve. They let the platform exist while we built toward the real target.

---

## Finally: wapka.org

In April 2020, wapka.org became available. This was different. A .org carries weight. It signals purpose over profit. It aligns with what Wapka actually is — open source, community-driven, built to serve, not to extract.

I registered it immediately. Four years after the original platform shut down, the brand had a home that felt legitimate.

---

## The domain that got away

Curious people sometimes ask: what about wapka.com?

It's still parked. Still owned by someone who doesn't use it. I check occasionally. It's not for sale at a number that makes sense.

And honestly — at this point, it doesn't matter. The brand is Wapka. The community is on wapka.org. The code is on GitHub. The domains we built around — .org, .site, .website — they carry the story of how the platform was rebuilt: not with a premium domain and venture backing, but with patience and whatever was available at the time.

---

## The lesson

You don't need the perfect domain to start. You need a domain that works, a product that delivers, and the persistence to keep building while the brand catches up.

The .com may come someday. Or it may not. Either way, Wapka is Wapka.
