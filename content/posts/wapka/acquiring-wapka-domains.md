Most founder stories begin with premium domains and venture backing. Mine began with a strategic alternative route.

---

## The original domain

The original Wapka ran on wapka.com and wapka.mobi. When the platform ended its run, those domains remained with the previous owner. I reached out early in the rebuild — 2018 or 2019 — and made an offer.

The asking price was $25,000 per domain. I had saved around $4,000 from small projects and platform contributions. The math didn't work.

Many would have stopped there. I chose a different path: build the brand from multiple domains, acquired over time as resources allowed.

---

## The piece-by-piece strategy

Rather than one premium domain, I secured the reachable alternatives — one at a time.

**wapka.co** came first. Then **wapka.site**. Then **wapka.website**. Each acquisition was a step forward. Each one proved the brand was active and growing.

| Domain | Registered |
|---|---|
| wapka.website | August 2018 |
| wapka.site | April 2019 |

A .co or .site might not carry the same instant recognition as a .com. But they work. They resolve. They let the platform exist and grow while we built toward the ideal.

---

## The turning point

In April 2020, wapka.org became available. A .org carries weight. It signals purpose and permanence. It aligns with what Wapka is: open source, community-driven, built to serve.

I registered it immediately. Four years of work, multiple domains, and finally — a home that felt definitive.

---

## Where it stands

wapka.com remains with its previous owner. I check occasionally. It's not for sale at a number that makes sense — and at this point, it doesn't need to be.

The brand is built. The community is on wapka.org. The code is on GitHub at `wapka-web`. The name is Wapka, and it stands on its own.

---

## The principle

You don't need perfection to start building. You need a working foundation, a product that delivers, and the persistence to keep growing while the brand catches up to the vision.

That's how Wapka was built — one decision, one domain, one iteration at a time.
